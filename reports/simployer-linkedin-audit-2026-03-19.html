Executive Summary

Simployer has a fragmented LinkedIn presence spread across 6+ pages (Group, Norge, Sverige, &frankly, AlexisHR, Tech Polska) that dilutes brand authority and splits follower growth. The Group page — the natural "home" — has only ~991 followers versus ~8,700 for Sverige and ~6,400 for Norge.

Content is overwhelmingly local-language, compliance-topic webinar promos — valuable for existing followers but invisible to the broader HR-tech audience. There is almost no English-language thought leadership, no technical storytelling, and the AI narrative (Sia, analytics, hackathons) appears in bursts without a sustained drumbeat.

The recent Purple Club community launch and brand refresh for the 40th anniversary are strong strategic moves that haven't yet translated into a distinctive LinkedIn content rhythm. The CPO departure (Caroline Fjellner, early 2026) creates both a leadership-voice gap and a moment to reset the narrative.

What's Working: Purple Club community-building, Sia product launch communications, local-market expert credibility (legal/compliance), 500 MNOK ARR milestone storytelling, hackathon culture visibility.
Critical Gaps: No unified English-language page strategy, near-zero AI thought leadership cadence, fragmented follower base across 6+ pages, content is event-driven rather than narrative-driven, no employee advocacy program visible, no founder/CEO personal brand presence at scale.
Key Opportunity: Simployer is repositioning as AI-first HR. LinkedIn is where Nordic HR decision-makers live. The gap between what Simployer is building (Sia, AI analytics, role-based dashboards) and what the LinkedIn presence communicates (webinar promos and compliance tips) is enormous. Closing this gap is a high-leverage move — especially with the website redesign landing soon.
Brand Messaging Analysis
💬

Tagline & Value Proposition

Mixed

The Group page tagline is "Our unique combination of HR Tech and local expertise, makes companies unleash the full potential in their people." This is a legacy positioning statement — it communicates breadth but not differentiation. Every HR-tech vendor claims to "unleash potential." The word "unique" does zero heavy lifting when not backed by specifics.

The Sverige page uses a Swedish variant of the same. Norge mirrors it in Norwegian. None of them mention AI, automation, Sia, compliance intelligence, or anything that would make a scrolling HR leader stop.

The careers site has a much stronger pitch: "combining HR processes, analytics, learning, compliance, and AI-driven capabilities in one seamless experience." This copy should migrate to LinkedIn — it's specific, modern, and closer to the actual product story.
🏷️

Visual Identity on LinkedIn

Weak

The purple brand refresh ("The Purple Club") is a strong visual signal, but it hasn't propagated across all LinkedIn pages. The Group page banner, profile imagery, and featured sections feel dated compared to the Purple Club community site, which is polished and modern.

The sub-brand pages (AlexisHR, &frankly) carry their own visual identities that don't visually connect to the Simployer parent brand. A visitor clicking between pages gets no sense of a unified platform.

🏆

Proof Points & Social Proof

Solid

Simployer has strong numbers that are underutilized on LinkedIn: 500 MNOK ARR, 40+ years in the market, 15,000+ customers, 1.2M users, operations in 4 countries. These aren't visible in the About section in a skimmable way. The 500 MNOK milestone got one post — it deserves to be a pinned Featured item and part of the ongoing narrative.

Content Audit Findings
📊

Content Mix & Frequency

Weak

The dominant content pattern across all Simployer pages is: webinar promo → compliance/legal topic → customer welcome → webinar promo. This is a lead-gen content calendar, not a thought-leadership strategy. It works for conversion but does nothing for brand reach or algorithmic distribution.

Post frequency appears inconsistent — long gaps followed by clusters around events or product launches. On the 2026 LinkedIn algorithm (360Brew), consistency and engagement velocity in the first hour are critical signals. Sporadic posting tanks organic reach.

  • ~70% of visible posts are webinar or event promotions
  • ~15% are customer announcements or internal celebrations
  • ~10% are product news (Sia launch, analytics, dashboards)
  • ~5% could be considered thought leadership
🌐

Language Strategy

Mixed

The local pages post almost exclusively in Norwegian/Swedish. The Group page mixes English and local languages inconsistently. This means there is no coherent English-language presence for international audiences, investors, potential hires, or the broader HR-tech conversation.

For a company with 500M+ NOK ARR and Nordic expansion ambitions, this is a significant visibility gap. International HR-tech media, analysts, and VC/PE audiences default to English on LinkedIn.

💡

Format & Engagement Quality

Weak

Posts are heavily link-based (linking to webinar sign-ups, blog posts, forms). LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes external links — they consistently get 40-60% less reach than native content. There's minimal use of the formats that drive 2026 engagement:

  • No carousel/document posts (which drive saves, the highest-weight engagement signal)
  • Very few native video posts (the hackathon and CPO/CTO video are exceptions)
  • No data visualizations, infographics, or framework posts
  • No polls, collaborative articles, or conversation starters
  • Hashtag strategy is generic (#hr #hrtech #simployer) — not niche enough for algorithmic topic matching
AI-First Positioning on LinkedIn

Sia — Product Launch Visibility

Mixed

The Sia AI assistant launch (April 2025) was communicated via a company blog post and amplified by individual sales reps like Christopher Robin Nordmo. The product page is solid. But the LinkedIn launch was a single-moment event, not a sustained narrative.

Compare this to how competitors like HiBob, Personio, or Rippling create multi-week launch campaigns with teaser content, customer testimonials, behind-the-scenes engineering posts, and founder commentary. Sia got one announcement cycle and then disappeared from the content calendar.

Sia is arguably Simployer's strongest competitive differentiator — an AI assistant integrated into the HRM with role-based responses, Teams/Slack integration, and escalation to human experts. This story is barely being told on LinkedIn. The December 2025 role-based dashboard launch and AI Insights Hub are also under-communicated.
🧠

AI Thought Leadership

Weak

There are scattered AI-adjacent posts: a blog about AI bias by a developer, a hackathon with AI focus, and a webinar on "how HR should think strategically about AI." But there is no visible, recurring AI thought leadership presence — no named AI lead posting regularly, no "building in public" engineering content, no opinion pieces on where HR AI is going.

The existing content frames AI as something Simployer is exploring, not something they're leading. The tone is cautious and educational rather than assertive and visionary. For a company that has actually shipped an AI product (Sia) integrated into an HRM used by 1.2M people, this undersells the reality significantly.

The Fragmentation Problem

Critical

Simployer's combined LinkedIn following across all pages is roughly ~21,000+. But the Group page — the only one that speaks to the company's full story — has less than 1,000 followers. This means:

  • Product announcements (Sia, dashboards, analytics) on the Group page reach almost nobody
  • International audiences have no meaningful entry point
  • The AI-first narrative has no "home base" with reach
  • Each sub-page builds its own isolated audience that never cross-pollinates
  • Acquired brands (AlexisHR, &frankly) maintain separate identities that compete with the parent brand
This is the single highest-impact structural problem. A content strategy can't compensate for an architecture where the most important messages go to the smallest audience.
🗺️

Recommended Architecture

Proposal

Two viable paths forward:

Option A — Consolidate to Group page: Migrate followers from local pages to the Group page through a coordinated campaign. Post in English as default with local-language content as supplementary. This is the "one brand, one page" approach that maximizes algorithmic reach per post. High effort, high payoff.

Option B — Hub-and-spoke: Keep local pages for local-language compliance content and event promotion. Elevate the Group page as the English-language "flagship" for product, AI, thought leadership, and employer brand content. Cross-promote aggressively. This preserves local follower equity while building the Group page. Medium effort, medium payoff.

Either way, the first move is the same: make the Group page worth following. Pin a featured section with the Sia story, 500M ARR milestone, and Purple Club. Update the tagline to reference AI. Start posting 2-3x/week in English with substance.

Identify 5 key voices (CEO, a product leader, a designer, an engineer, a customer success lead). Give them a loose brief: 1 post per week, personal perspective on what they're building or learning. Sia's AI Insights Hub aggregates what managers and employees ask the chatbot. This is a goldmine for original data content. Monthly LinkedIn posts with anonymized trending questions across Nordic workplaces would be highly shareable and position Simployer as having unique data access others don't.

Medium Purple Club → LinkedIn content pipeline

Member spotlights, event recaps, discussion highlights, and "HR hot takes" from Purple Club sessions should flow back to the Group page as native content. The community is generating conversations — capture and redistribute them.

Medium Unify visual identity across all pages

Update banners, logos, and About sections across all 6+ pages to reflect the Purple brand refresh. Right now a visitor hopping between pages gets a disjointed experience. Even if pages stay separate, they should visually feel like one family.

Stop posting webinar links as the main content. Instead: write the insight as a native text post, put the link in the first comment. Or create a carousel summarizing the webinar topic and mention registration in the comments. This alone can 2-3x organic reach per post.
